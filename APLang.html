AP Language and Composition:

This class is very writing intensive and the exam will definetly have your hand broken as it consists of 45 multiple choice questions (for the 2020 exam) and 3 free response questions (synthesis, analysis, and argumentative). While many say this exam is one of the hardest, it won't be as long as you study the correct techniques needed to write each essay, vocab terms, and take multiple choice practice questions so that you can finish each reading passage on time and correctly. To help you with that, here are some of my recommendations so that you can be successful on the exam.

Review Books

5 Steps to a 5 by Barbara L. Murphy and Estelle M. Rankin

This is the book my teacher used and she said it helped guide her whenever
she was teaching us, which is why it is the only book she would use if she
were us. If you are someone that needs a review book no matter what exam
you're taking, then this is for you. The AP Lang exam was not an exam
that I used a review book for, but that's because I had really good class notes
and didn't feel the need to purchase one. If you are looking fort a resource
though, I highly recommend this one because according to my tecaher and
other students who did use this book, it is concise and has strategies for how
to approach each essay along with practice essays and multiple choice questions.
It also has a glossary with useful terms in it, which I feel is particularly necessary
for this exam since you need to know the terminology and rhetorical terms to
include them in your essay (especially during the analysis essay).
