AP Calculus:

AP Calculus AB and AP Calculus BC focuses on the understanding of calculus concepts with methods and applications. In both AB and BC it requires the use of definitions and theorems in order to answer questions. While the course can come naturally to some people, it may be harder to understand for others (like myself). The test consists of 45 multiple choice questions and 6 frq's that are mostly AB topics. In order to help you be successful, there are many practice questions that can be found in review books and the internet, some of these resources can be found below.

Review Books

Cracking the AP Calculus AB Exam by Princeton Review.

This Princeton review book has all the topics and practice questions
you need to help you suceed in AP Calculus and better prepare you for the exam.
This review book includes a comprehensive studyguide that includes three
full length, thourough content, and access to the online AP connect portal.
This book is one of the best for Calculus review and the difficulty level is
pretty comparable to the actual exam. I would recommend this book over the
Barron's book as the Barron's book is a lot harder than the actual exam.
